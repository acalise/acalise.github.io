15 Word Games for Adults That Actually Challenge You

Most "word games for adults" on the App Store are just kids' games with a darker UI. Real talk. These are the ones that actually stretch a grown-up vocabulary. A mix of apps, daily puzzles, old-school paper formats, and a few party games worth hauling out at dinner.

Daily Digital Puzzles

1. NYT Crossword (Thursday–Saturday)

Monday is easy, Saturday is brutal. For adult difficulty, skip Mon-Wed. Saturday crosswords are the gold standard of word-puzzle hardness.

2. Spelling Bee

Finding every valid word from 7 letters is an exhaustive search problem. Getting to "Genius" level daily requires real vocabulary breadth.

3. Word Walk (hard puzzles)

The curated ladders at higher difficulty levels require 10+ step transformations. You'll hit words you haven't used in years. Word Walk.

4. Connections

The purple category is designed to humble you. Lateral thinking, misdirection, and the occasional pun that makes you laugh.

5. Contexto / Semantle

Semantic guessing games. Contexto gives you a rank. Semantle just tells you how close you are in embedding space. Hard in a completely different way.

Apps

6. SpellTower

Rising tiles, word-search mechanics, time pressure. Puzzle mode alone is deep enough for months.

7. Knotwords

A crossword with no clues. You fill shaded regions to form valid words. Zach Gage's best puzzle game.

8. Scrabble Go / Words With Friends

Asynchronous multiplayer Scrabble. The right partner makes this the best word game on mobile.

9. 7 Little Words (Impossible difficulty)

The daily Impossible puzzles are genuinely hard. Perfect 5-minute brain snack.

A daily ladder for adults

Word Walk scales from casual to genuinely hard. The curated advanced ladders hit the sweet spot for experienced solvers.

Paper and Books

10. Cryptic Crosswords (The Guardian, The Times)

Cryptic crosswords are the hardest word puzzle format in the world. Every clue is a riddle with wordplay. If you solve your first cryptic unaided, you will feel like a genius.

11. The Enigma (National Puzzlers' League)

A monthly magazine of verbal puzzles used to train the world's best puzzle-solvers. Sign up if you're ready for the big leagues.

12. Will Shortz Word Ladders books

Classic word ladder collections, 200+ puzzles per volume. The physical format is a nice change of pace from apps.

Group / Party Word Games

13. Bananagrams

Speed Scrabble without the board. Brutally fast and addictive with 2-8 players.

14. Just One (party game)

Cooperative word-guessing. Each teammate writes a clue for the same target word. Duplicate clues get cancelled. Hilarious and surprisingly hard.

15. Codenames

Word association under team pressure. The best strategic word game of the last decade. Sober play only, it rewards careful thinking.

How to Pick

Match the format to your attention. 5-minute commute: Wordle, Letter Boxed, Word Walk. 15-minute coffee: Crossword, Spelling Bee, ladder archives. Long weekend: Cryptic crossword. Dinner with friends: Codenames, Just One.

Quick Takeaways

  • Real difficulty lives in NYT Saturday crosswords, Spelling Bee Genius, and cryptic crosswords.
  • Word Walk hits the adult sweet spot with curated advanced ladders.
  • For social play: Codenames, Bananagrams, Just One.
  • For slow burn: Cryptics and The Enigma.
