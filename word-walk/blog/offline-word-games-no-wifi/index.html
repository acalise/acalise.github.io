Best Offline Word Games (No Wi-Fi Needed) for iPhone in 2026

There's nothing worse than opening your favorite word game on a plane and getting a "no internet connection" splash screen. Wordle, in particular, is brutal at 30,000 feet. So here's a tested list of word games that actually work offline, in airplane mode, on subways, in basements, and anywhere internet isn't.

Why Most Daily Word Games Need the Internet

Most daily word games — Wordle, Connections, Strands, Spelling Bee — pull their puzzle from a server each day. The server has the answer; your phone doesn't. That's how they enforce "everyone gets the same puzzle." It also means: airplane mode breaks them.

Offline word games solve this by either (a) storing the puzzle catalog on-device, or (b) generating puzzles locally from an on-device dictionary. Both work in airplane mode. Both work in subway tunnels. Both work in your friend's terrible-Wi-Fi cabin.

1. Word Walk — Daily Word Ladders, Offline

Word ladders (change one letter at a time) work great offline because each puzzle is just two words and a dictionary check. Word Walk caches puzzles locally after first launch, so you get the daily word ladder plus the back catalog with no signal. Word ladders were Lewis Carroll's invention and predate the internet by 110 years — fitting that they still work without it.

2. Word Search Apps

The classic. Find words in a letter grid. Most word search apps store hundreds of puzzles on-device after install. Look for ones with adjustable grid size and minimal ads. The format is forgiving, the puzzles are bottomless, and the difficulty scales smoothly.

3. Crossword Apps with Downloaded Packs

Most paid crossword apps (NYT Crossword, Shortyz, Crossword Solver) let you download a week or month of puzzles in advance. You play offline; sync happens later. Pre-download before a long flight and you're set for hours.

4. 7 Little Words / Words of Wonders

Both ship hundreds of puzzles in the install. Both run in airplane mode. Both have surprisingly elegant difficulty curves — easy enough to feel productive, hard enough to challenge.

5. Bananagrams (Physical, But Counts)

The bag of letter tiles is the ultimate offline word game. Two players, no electricity, no signal. Lives in your bag forever. Beats anything on a phone for shared travel time.

6. Boggle Apps

Shake the cubes, find words in three minutes. Most Boggle clones generate the cube tray locally, which means: full offline play. The official Boggle With Friends needs a connection, but solo Boggle apps work anywhere.

7. Anagram Apps

Given a set of letters, find words. The classic format. Apps like Word Cookies, Word Connect, and Anagram have on-device dictionaries — they don't need a server. Some show ads that need internet, so airplane mode actually improves the experience.

8. Hangman Apps

Solo Hangman against a phone-generated word list. Works fully offline. Honestly, you can also play this on a napkin.

9. Cryptogram Apps

Decode a sentence where each letter has been swapped. Pure logic, fully offline, oddly meditative. A long flight is the perfect setting.

10. Word Cookies / Word Connect Variants

Free apps with hundreds of bake-themed offline levels. Touch tiles to form words from given letters. The free versions are ad-supported, but ads only load with a connection — airplane mode skips them.

How to Verify a Word Game Works Offline

Don't trust App Store descriptions. Test it:

  1. Install the app while on Wi-Fi.
  2. Open it once and play through one puzzle (this triggers any first-run downloads).
  3. Force-quit the app.
  4. Enable airplane mode.
  5. Re-open the app and try to play.

If it works, you're set. If you see a "no connection" message, find another app.

What Doesn't Work Offline

Skip these for plane play:

  • Wordle — fetches the daily word from the NYT server.
  • Connections — same.
  • Strands, Spelling Bee, NYT Mini — same.
  • Words With Friends multiplayer — needs your opponent.
  • Anything with "online" or "tournament" in the name.

Pre-Flight Word Game Checklist

Before a long flight, run through this list:

  1. Open every word game in your collection at least once on Wi-Fi.
  2. For crossword apps, download a puzzle pack manually.
  3. For Word Walk, complete one daily puzzle to confirm the cache.
  4. Toggle airplane mode and verify each game still launches.
  5. Make sure your AirPods are charged. (Unrelated, but trust me.)

Quick Takeaways

  • Word ladders, word searches, anagrams, and crosswords with downloaded packs all work offline.
  • Wordle, Connections, Strands, and Spelling Bee do not work offline.
  • Open every offline game once on Wi-Fi before takeoff.
  • Word Walk caches puzzles locally for weeks of airplane-mode play.
  • Test airplane mode at home before relying on a game in flight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there word games that work without internet?

Yes — word ladders, word searches, anagrams, and offline crossword apps all work without an internet connection. Wordle and most "daily" games do not.

What word games work in airplane mode?

Anything that stores its puzzles on-device. Word Walk, Boggle clones, word search apps, and pre-downloaded crossword packs all work in airplane mode.

How do I find offline word games on the App Store?

Search "offline word games" and check descriptions for "play offline" or "no Wi-Fi required." Test by toggling airplane mode after install.

Does Word Walk work offline?

Yes — Word Walk caches puzzles on-device after first launch, so daily word ladders run in airplane mode and anywhere with no signal.
