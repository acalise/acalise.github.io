The Best Free Word Games With No Ads in 2026

Most "free" word games are really ads with a puzzle tacked on. The part that annoyed me was realizing how short the real no-ad list actually is. Here are the genuinely free word games that don't interrupt you with a video every 30 seconds. Short list, and that's kind of the whole point.

The Honest Truth About "Free" Word Games

Almost every top-grossing word game on the App Store is free-to-play ad-supported. That's the business model. You play a puzzle, you watch an ad. You watch an ad, you get a hint. You get stuck, you watch more ads or pay. The ads are the product.

A smaller group of word games charge upfront, a one-time purchase to remove ads, or run on a subscription. These are the ones where the puzzle is the product.

Fully Free, Zero Ads

Wordle (nytimes.com/games/wordle)

Still completely free on the web, with no ads during gameplay. You can't beat free plus no ads. The only catch is that the NYT is increasingly pushing subscriptions for the rest of their games.

Letter Boxed (NYT Games)

Free on the web and in the NYT Games app. No ads. Daily puzzle, clever format. One of the best deals in mobile gaming.

Squaredle

Free in-browser daily word grid. No ads, no account required. Made by an indie developer who clearly loves the format.

Contexto

Free semantic guessing game. No ads. Great for players who want a completely different take on the daily puzzle.

Free to Play, Ads You Can Kill

These games are free to install and include ads, but offer a cheap one-time purchase that kills ads permanently. Fair trade.

Word Walk ($1.99 one-time)

Free to install with a daily word ladder puzzle and hundreds more. The one-time $1.99 IAP removes ads permanently. No subscription. Our app.

Knotwords ($4.99 one-time, no ads ever)

Paid upfront, no ads, no subscription. Zach Gage's excellent crossword-adjacent puzzle game. The clean version of "free to play."

SpellTower+ ($9.99 one-time)

One of the all-time-best word games. Paid version has no ads. Also available via Apple Arcade.

Subscription Only (NYT Games)

The NYT Games subscription ($5/mo) gives you the full Wordle archive, Spelling Bee, Connections, Strands, the Crossword, and more. No ads, ever. If you do dailies, it's the best word-game money you can spend.

Word Walk: free with a one-time ad remover

Free daily word ladders. $1.99 to remove ads forever. No subscription, no energy bars, no dark patterns.

Games to Skip

We won't name individual apps, but a rule of thumb: if a "free" word game has any of the following, skip it:

  • Energy bars that limit how many puzzles you can play.
  • Video ads between every puzzle.
  • Coin shops where you can buy hints with real money.
  • "Lives" that regenerate over 3 hours unless you watch an ad.
  • Pop-ups begging for reviews after every win.

These are all signs the game is optimized to maximize ad impressions, not puzzle quality.

Quick Takeaways

  • Truly free + no ads is rare. NYT Games (web) and Squaredle lead the category.
  • The fair middle: free to install, one-time ad-removal purchase under $5.
  • NYT Games subscription is the best all-in-one if you do daily puzzles.
  • Skip anything with energy bars, coin shops, or ads between every puzzle.
