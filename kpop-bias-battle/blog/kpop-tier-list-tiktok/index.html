How to Make a Shareable K-Pop Tier List for TikTok and Instagram

K-pop tier lists are one of the most reliably engagement-friendly post formats on TikTok and Instagram. Millions of tier-list posts circulate every month under tags like #kpoptierlist and #kpopbiaslist, and any fandom you can name has a dedicated subset of accounts that exist primarily to post them. Here's how to make one that doesn't get scrolled past.

Step 1: Pick Your Scope

Scope is the single most important decision. The right scope makes the list feel definitive. The wrong one makes it feel random. Your options, ranked roughly by how broadly they travel:

  • One specific group. Easiest to make, easiest for fans of that group to engage with. Smaller potential audience but high engagement-per-post.
  • One generation. "4th gen girl groups," "5th gen boy groups." Bigger audience, more arguments in the comments (which TikTok rewards).
  • By gender. "Top 20 girl group members," "Best boy group bias." Broad reach, fewer niche fans but lots of cross-fandom views.
  • All-time / all-gen. The trophy format. Hardest to rank, most controversial, maximum reach when it lands.

If you're new to posting, start with a single group. Master that, then go broader.

Step 2: Do the Actual Ranking

Don't drag-and-drop from memory. Run a sorter first. We covered why in detail in the sorter vs tier list breakdown, but the short version: pairwise comparison reveals preferences you can't articulate, and the ELO math produces a ranking that's actually defensible when commenters challenge it.

Step 3: Export the Right Artifact

You have three useful exports for posting:

The Top 10 Share Card

Step 4: Get the Sizing Right

The fastest way to lose engagement is wrong aspect ratios. Some quick targets:

  • Instagram feed: 1080x1350 (4:5 vertical). Stop using square crops — they waste space and look smaller in feed.
  • Instagram Stories / Reels: 1080x1920 (9:16 full vertical).
  • TikTok: 1080x1920 (9:16). TikTok hard-letterboxes anything else, which kills polish.
  • Twitter / X: 1200x675 or any 16:9 horizontal works. K-pop Twitter is the one place where horizontal still performs.

Step 5: Write the Caption

Captions on tier-list posts do most of the engagement work. The pattern that works:

  1. Lead with the scope and a take. "My honest 4th gen girl group bias list. Fight me."
  2. Acknowledge controversy. "I know putting [X] in B tier is illegal. I stand by it."
  3. Invite participation. "Drop your tier list in the comments."
  4. Hashtag intelligently. Use group-specific tags (#blackpinkbiaslist), generic ones (#kpoptierlist, #kpopbias), and one trending sound or term if there's a fresh one.

Caption Templates That Work

  • "my [group] tier list as of [month]. ranked using a sorter so don't @ me with vibes 😭"
  • "i refuse to apologize for my bias placement. who is yours?"
  • "sorted [group] members head to head. the bias spotlight reveal made me cry."
  • "4th gen vs 5th gen girl groups: my final tier list. screaming at my own A tier."

Step 6: Post at the Right Time

K-pop content spikes in two daily windows: around 8–10 AM Korean time (when comebacks drop) and 7–11 PM in your local timezone (when international fans are home). Tier lists don't ride comeback waves, so the local-evening window is where you want to be posting.

Avoid posting against major comeback teasers from the group you're ranking — the fandom is too distracted to engage with your list. Check the comeback calendar before you post.

Common Mistakes That Tank Posts

  • F-tiering active idols. Reads as antifandom. Most viral tier lists either skip F entirely or use it for a clearly joking placement.
  • No visible names. If you use a custom design without member names, casual fans can't engage. Include them.
  • Tiny faces. If your tier list has 50 members crammed in, faces are unrecognizable. Either go smaller scope or split into multiple slides.
  • No context. Posting just the image with no caption depresses comments. Always frame it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size should a K-pop tier list be for Instagram?

For Instagram feed, a 1080x1350 vertical image works best. For Stories and Reels, use 1080x1920. Square crops (1080x1080) are fine for feed but waste space on tier-list grids.

What size should a K-pop tier list be for TikTok?

TikTok displays everything at 1080x1920 vertical. Build your tier list to that aspect ratio so it fills the screen. Square images get letterboxed and look smaller than they should.

How do I make my K-pop tier list go viral?

You can't guarantee virality, but the patterns that perform best are: clear group/scope in the title, your bias visible immediately, a specific take in the caption, and posting during peak fandom hours (afternoons local time).

Is it bad to put an idol in F tier?

Most K-pop tier lists skip F tier entirely or use it humorously. F-tiering an active idol you follow can read as antifandom behavior. If you're going to post, keep things either positive or playful, not harsh.

Can I make a multi-group K-pop tier list?

Yes, and these often perform better than single-group lists because they appeal to more fandoms at once. Generation tier lists (4th gen, 5th gen) and gender tier lists (girl group, boy group) are the most popular formats.
