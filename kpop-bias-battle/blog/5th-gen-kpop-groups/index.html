Every 5th Gen K-Pop Group You Should Know in 2026

The 5th generation is the youngest, fastest-moving, and most TikTok-native wave of K-pop yet. If you're trying to keep up but the debuts blur together, this is your cheat sheet. One paragraph per group, sorted roughly by recognition. For the broader context on what counts as 5th gen, check our K-pop generations explainer.

The Big-Label 5th Gen Groups

ZEROBASEONE (ZB1)

A 9-member boy group from WAKEONE Entertainment, formed through the Mnet survival show Boys Planet in 2023. Members include Sung Hanbin, Kim Jiwoong, Zhang Hao, Sung Hanbin, Seok Matthew, Kim Taerag, Ricky, Kim Gyuvin, Park Gunwook, and Han Yujin. ZB1 is technically a project group with a contracted activity period, which gives their releases an urgency other groups don't have. Their sound is bright, harmony-forward boy-group pop. Start with "In Bloom" and "Sweat."

RIIZE

SM Entertainment's first boy group debut in years, RIIZE arrived in 2023 with seven members (Shotaro, Eunseok, Sungchan, Wonbin, Seunghan, Sohee, Anton). Their angle is "real-time pop" — songs designed to feel friendly and unpretentious. "Get a Guitar," "Talk Saxy," and "Boom Boom Bass" are all clean entry points. The group has had real-world drama around member statuses, which has shifted lineups, but the core appeal — easy melodies, sharp choreo — is intact.

BABYMONSTER

YG Entertainment's first girl group since BLACKPINK. Seven members (Ruka, Pharita, Asa, Ahyeon, Rami, Rora, Chiquita) chosen through an extensive evaluation process. Debuted late 2023 / early 2024 with "Batter Up" and "Sheesh." Their lane is heavier hip-hop and rap-forward pop in YG's house style, but with a tighter, more vocally diverse sound than their predecessors.

TWS

HYBE/PLEDIS's boy group debut after SEVENTEEN. Six members (Shinyu, Dohoon, Youngjae, Hanjin, Jihoon, Kyungmin), branded as "the boyhood next door." Debuted in January 2024 with "Plot Twist," which immediately became a TikTok hit. Their concept is deliberately approachable — songs about school, crushes, and growing up, with airy production. If you bounced off heavier 4th gen aesthetics, TWS is the welcome mat.

ILLIT

BELIFT LAB's five-member girl group (Yunah, Minju, Moka, Wonhee, Iroha) that debuted in March 2024 with "Magnetic." The single went viral on TikTok almost instantly. Their sound is firmly in the post-NewJeans easy-listening pop lane — soft vocals, minimalist production, big choruses you can sing along to without trying. "Cherish (My Love)" and "Magnetic" are the must-listens.

The Critically Acclaimed and Rising

KISS OF LIFE

S2 Entertainment's four-member girl group (Julie, Natty, Belle, Haneul) that debuted in 2023. Considered one of the most vocally talented 5th gen groups. Their sound leans R&B and old-school US-pop, which has earned them serious respect from fans who care about technique. "Bad News," "Sticky," and "Midas Touch" showcase the range.

BOYNEXTDOOR

HYBE/KOZ Entertainment's six-member boy group (Sungho, Riwoo, Jaehyun, Taesan, Leehan, Woonhak), debuted in 2023 under producer Zico. Their songs feel like indie-pop and pop-rock crossed with K-pop melodic sensibility — guitar-forward, lyrically grounded, less heavily-produced than typical 4th gen boy group output. "But Sometimes," "Earth, Wind and Fire," and "Serenade" are good starting points.

NMIXX

Technically late 4th gen by debut date (2022) but often grouped with 5th gen in vibes and audience overlap. Six members (Lily, Haewon, Sullyoon, Bae, Jiwoo, Kyujin) from JYP. NMIXX's signature is "mixx pop" — songs that shift genre mid-track, defying the typical K-pop verse-chorus structure. "DICE" and "O.O" both pull this off in radically different ways.

&TEAM

HYBE LABELS Japan's nine-member multinational boy group. Debuted late 2022 in Japan, but their 5th-gen-adjacent sound and timing put them in the conversation. Members from Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. Their sound is melodic and slightly darker than ENHYPEN's, their direct sibling group at HYBE. "Under the Skin" and "Aoarashi" are starter tracks.

Smaller But Growing

FIFTY FIFTY

Debuted early 2023 with "Cupid," which became one of the biggest K-pop viral hits ever on TikTok. The group went through a public legal dispute with their label, which restructured the lineup. Their early discography is still worth a listen — minimalist Western pop with breathy vocals.

tripleS

Modhaus's enormous experimental group — at 24 members, the most ambitious 5th gen project on the table. Members can be voted into different sub-units by fans through a blockchain-linked app called Cosmo. Their full-group song "Rising" debuted with all 24 onstage. tripleS proves that the "more members, more sub-units" model still has room to grow, just with weirder mechanics. If you want to bias-pick across 24 members, we wrote about how to choose a bias in large groups.

ARTMS

Five-member girl group of former LOONA members (HeeJin, HaSeul, KimLip, JinSoul, Choerry) under Modhaus. Officially "5th gen" by debut but in practice an experienced-veterans act with a built-in fandom. Their sound is dreamy, alt-pop-leaning, and quite different from the rest of 5th gen.

UNIS, ME:I, IS:SUE, IZNA

Multiple survival-show-born girl groups debuted across 2024 (UNIS from Universe Ticket, ME:I from PRODUCE 101 Japan, IS:SUE from No-No-Girls, IZNA from I-LAND 2). Each has a specific niche audience but together they represent the proliferation of the project-group model.

What Makes 5th Gen Distinctive

A few observations about the wave as a whole:

  • Smaller lineups. Where 3rd gen averaged 7–13 members, 5th gen averages 4–7. Easier to learn, easier to market, less rotation between sub-units.
  • TikTok-first marketing. Most 5th gen songs are designed with a TikTok hook in mind. The 15-second loop is the unit of viral spread, and labels know it.
  • Easy-listening lane. NewJeans, ILLIT, and TWS all prove that minimalist, soft-vocal pop is the dominant 5th gen aesthetic — though aggressive sounds (BABYMONSTER, ATEEZ-adjacent groups) still have a lane.
  • Survival show roots. ZB1, BABYMONSTER, IZNA, and many others came from survival shows. This is now the default debut path, not the exception.
  • Direct-to-fan platforms. Bubble and Weverse messaging are the standard. Fans pay monthly for direct text-style updates from idols, which has reshaped engagement expectations.

Who's Still Coming

New 5th gen groups continue to debut multiple times per quarter. By the time this article is six months old, there will be 4–8 new acts on the table. The smart move is to follow a few specific groups closely rather than try to absorb everything — pick your bias group and let the rest filter in through TikTok.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 5th gen K-pop group?

A 5th generation K-pop group is one that debuted from roughly 2023 onward. The 5th gen is defined more by sound, marketing approach, and the post-NewJeans landscape than by any official cutoff date.

Is NewJeans 4th gen or 5th gen?

NewJeans debuted in 2022, which most fans classify as late 4th generation. Some consider them the bridge into 5th gen because their minimalist sound influenced everything that came after.

What is the biggest 5th gen group right now?

Popularity shifts comeback to comeback, but RIIZE, ILLIT, BABYMONSTER, and ZEROBASEONE have been the most consistently charting 5th gen groups since their debuts. TWS has also grown into a major boy group act.

Which 5th gen group should I get into first?

If you like minimalist, easy-listening pop, try ILLIT or TWS. If you like bigger-concept dance music, try ZEROBASEONE or BABYMONSTER. If you want vocal-driven group dynamics, RIIZE and KISS OF LIFE are great entry points.

How many members do 5th gen groups have?

Most 5th gen groups are smaller than their predecessors, with four to seven members being most common. The shrinking lineup helps groups be more nimble on social media and easier for fans to learn quickly.
