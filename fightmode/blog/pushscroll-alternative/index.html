What Is Pushscroll?

Pushscroll is an exercise-to-unlock app: it shields the apps that eat your day and only reopens them once you do pushups or squats. The signature model is roughly one pushup for one minute of access - a clean, honest trade. Do the reps, earn the scroll.

It works. Charging a physical toll to open Instagram is far stickier than a friction pause you can tap away. The reason people still look for an alternative isn't that the concept is broken - it's that the specific task or model doesn't fit everyone.

Why People Look for an Alternative

  • Pushups get boring. The same rep, dozens of times a day, loses its bite. Boredom is the number-one way any blocker gets uninstalled.
  • Pricing. Some people want a free option or a plan that fits differently.
  • They want a different exercise. Not everyone wants to drop and do pushups - in public, in work clothes, or with a wrist injury, another movement is easier.
  • The timer-vs-budget model can feel fiddly. Some users find earning and spending minutes harder to track than a simpler unlock.

If any of those is you, the good news is the category has grown a lot. Here are the alternatives worth your time.

FightMode: The Most Engaging Unlock

FightMode is the boxing answer to Pushscroll. Instead of reps, your apps stay locked until you win a 60-second boxing or kickboxing round. A coach calls the combos out loud, and an AI scorecard grades your technique when the bell rings.

That design directly targets Pushscroll's one weakness: repetition. A rep counter is the same task every time. A live round with fresh combos and a real score is different every time - which is exactly what keeps you doing it on unlock number 200 instead of quitting in week two. It also means the friction doubles as real shadowboxing practice, so combat-sports people get training they'd want anyway. If that's you, it's built as a boxing app blocker first.

Repscroll: for a firmer lock

Repscroll keeps the rep model but adds a hard-lock mode for people who need the door bolted, plus an emergency-scroll escape for the moments you genuinely need in. If your complaint with Pushscroll is that it's too easy to cave, Repscroll tightens the screws.

FitBlok: for variety

FitBlok spreads the task across pushups, squats, jumping jacks, sit-ups, and planks. If pushups specifically are what wore you out, rotating exercises keeps the unlock fresher than a single movement.

EarnScroll: for free

If price is the whole reason you're switching, EarnScroll delivers the core exercise-to-unlock loop at no cost. Fewer bells and whistles, but the fundamental trade - reps for access - is intact.
